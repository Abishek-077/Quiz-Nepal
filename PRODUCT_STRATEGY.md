# Quiz Nepal Product Strategy

## Target users

- **Driving license learners:** want realistic written-exam practice without reading long PDFs.
- **Loksewa and GK students:** need repeated questions, clear explanations, and weak-topic review.
- **Nepali students and job seekers:** want quick practice during short breaks.
- **Casual learners:** want Fun/IQ questions that build habit before deeper exam preparation.

The core pain is boredom plus uncertainty. Users do not just need questions; they need fast feedback, confidence, and a reason to return tomorrow.

## MVP positioning

Quiz Nepal should feel like a useful exam-prep battle, not a generic quiz app. The first promise is:

> Choose a category, answer 10 questions, learn why, and beat your previous score.

The MVP avoids dark patterns. It uses progress, streaks, coins, and leaderboard status as healthy motivation rather than fake scarcity or misleading pressure.

## Funnel

1. **Content / landing page:** publish short Nepal GK, driving-license, and Loksewa-style question posts.
2. **App download / open:** simple value promise, no forced account creation in MVP.
3. **Choose exam category:** Driving License, Loksewa GK, Nepal GK, Computer/IT GK, Fun/IQ Quiz.
4. **Take first quiz within 30 seconds:** no complex onboarding.
5. **See score and explanations:** deliver learning value immediately.
6. **Invite into retention loop:** streak, coins, weak topics, leaderboard, try again.
7. **Monetize later:** ads and premium only after the user has received clear value.

## First-session conversion principles

- Remove friction: no signup wall, no long tutorial.
- Show clear category cards based on real user goals.
- Keep quiz length to 10 questions so completion feels achievable.
- Give immediate feedback after every answer.
- End with a friendly result, review items, and a clear “Try again and beat your score” CTA.

## Retention loops

- **Daily quiz streak:** encourages return tomorrow without punishment language.
- **Coins:** rewards completed learning, not gambling behavior.
- **Weak topics:** makes mistakes useful and gives the user a reason to continue.
- **Best scores:** motivates self-improvement by category.
- **Leaderboard:** provides social proof and competition while staying lightweight.
- **Share score / question:** supports organic content loops and future deep links.

## Trust psychology

Users trust the app when it teaches clearly. Every quiz answer should include:

- Correct answer.
- Short explanation.
- Category and topic.
- Difficulty.
- Review marking when wrong.

The product should never claim guaranteed exam success. It should position itself as practice, repetition, and confidence-building.

## Monetization plan

### Free MVP

- Banner ad placeholder after category selection/profile value.
- Interstitial ad placeholder only after every 3 completed quizzes.
- Rewarded ad placeholder for future hint or extra life.

### Premium roadmap

- No ads.
- Full mock tests.
- Detailed AI explanations.
- Exam-specific question packs.
- Progress analytics by topic and category.

Monetization should be delayed until the user understands the product value. The app should never block core learning in the first session.

## Content marketing plan

- Daily “Can you answer this?” Nepal GK cards.
- Driving license traffic-sign and rule explainers.
- Loksewa repeated-question threads with short explanations.
- City/province leaderboard campaigns.
- Deep links for each category and campaign slug.
- User result shares: “I scored 8/10 in Nepal GK. Can you beat me?”

## Metrics to validate

- Time from app open to first quiz start.
- First quiz completion rate.
- Explanation view rate.
- Day-1 and Day-7 return rate.
- Repeat quiz rate by category.
- Weak-topic review engagement.
- Share button taps.
- Ad/premium conversion only after retention is proven.
